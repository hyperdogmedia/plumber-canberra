Ways Professional Plumbers Can Help With a Blocked Drain

When you're dealing with blocked drains, licensed plumbing experts have professional tools and equipment to find and fix the blockage properly. As an example, they can use hydro jetting to flush your pipes.

A drainage auger is a professional device consisting of a flexible cable with a grabber attachment that can remove hair and other clogs. Many plumbers also use a motorized auger for tough blockages.

Inspection
Blocked drains can be a major problem for any resident. Apart from being annoying and causing odours, they can develop into a health risk if ignored.

Be aware of these telltale signs: foul odours, slow-draining water, and gurgling sounds from your plumbing.

While you might consider trying some simple fixes first, severe blockages need professional help. A plumbing expert may need to check the street side plug and use a CCTV to find the source of the clog.

Using a Plunger
A sink plunger (also known as a force cup) is a essential tool for unblocking drain blockages. For best results, start by running the plunger under warm water to make it more flexible and improve suction.

Advanced Solutions
For persistent clogs, professional plumbers might use water jetting. This advanced technique uses pressurized water to thoroughly clean your drain lines.

Keep in mind that while chemical cleaners are available from stores, they can be harsh to your pipes. It's safer to reach out to a licensed plumber for persistent problems.
