4. Frozen Pipe

A risk for frozen pipes which is a result of the frigid winter temperatures. Frozen pipes can lead to numerous issues, from water damage to flooding. The homeowners can keep a pipe from becoming frozen by recognizing the warning indicators.

If you suspect the existence of a frozen pipe, close your home's main water supply valve. This will decrease the pressure, thereby preventing the pipe from breaking. Next, turn off any faucets serviced by the affected pipe. This will ease pressure on the pipe. It will also allow it to defrost it.

Apply heat to the frozen section of the pipe by using a space heater, heat lamp, or hair dryer. Avoid using a blowtorch propane or kerosene heating device, or charcoal stove, as it could cause fire.

Ways Professional Plumbers Can Help With a Blocked Drain

When you're experiencing clogged pipes, professional plumbers have special tools and equipment to locate and clear the blockage efficiently. For instance, they can use hydro jetting to flush your pipes.

A plumber's snake is a professional device consisting of a long cable with a grabber attachment that can remove buildup and other obstructions. Many plumbers also use a mechanical auger for tough blockages.

Initial Assessment
Drain blockages can be a major problem for any homeowner. Besides being inconvenient and producing unpleasant odours, they can become a safety concern if left untreated.

Look for these warning signs: bad smells, slow-draining water, and unusual noises from your plumbing.

While you might consider trying some simple fixes first, serious blockages need expert assistance. A licensed professional may need to examine the street side plug and use a video inspection to identify the origin of the obstruction.

Plunger Solutions
A sink plunger (also known as a force cup) is a essential tool for clearing drain blockages. For maximum efficiency, first placing the plunger under hot water to make it more flexible and create a better seal.

Advanced Solutions
For stubborn blockages, licensed technicians might use water jetting. This advanced technique uses high-pressure water to completely clear your drain lines.

Note that while drain cleaning products are available from shops, they can be harmful to your pipes. It's more effective to contact a professional plumber for major clogs.
