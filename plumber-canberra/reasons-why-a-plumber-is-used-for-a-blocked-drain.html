Ways Professional Plumbers Can Help With a Blocked Drain

When you're dealing with blocked drains, professional plumbers have special equipment and tools to find and fix the blockage efficiently. For example, they can use hydro jetting to flush your pipes.

A plumber's snake is a professional device consisting of a flexible cable with a grabber fitting that can remove hair and other clogs. Most plumbers also use a mechanical auger for tough blockages.

Professional Evaluation
Drain blockages can be a serious hassle for any homeowner. In addition to being frustrating and producing unpleasant odours, they can develop into a health hazard if not addressed.

Watch out for these warning signs: foul odours, slow-draining water, and unusual noises from your drains.

While you might consider trying some DIY solutions first, severe blockages need professional help. A licensed professional may need to inspect the street side plug and use a camera to find the cause of the obstruction.

Plunging Techniques
A plunger (also known as a plumber's friend) is a basic tool for clearing drain blockages. For best results, start by placing the plunger under hot water to soften the rubber and create a better seal.

Advanced Solutions
For stubborn blockages, professional plumbers might use water jetting. This professional method uses pressurized water to thoroughly clean your pipes.

Keep in mind that while chemical cleaners are readily available from shops, they can be damaging to your pipes. It's safer to contact a professional plumber for major clogs.
