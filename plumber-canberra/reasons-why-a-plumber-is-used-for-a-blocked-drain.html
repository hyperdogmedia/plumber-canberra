Ways Professional Plumbers Can Help With a Blocked Drain

When you're facing drain blockages, licensed plumbing experts have specialized tools and equipment to find and fix the problem properly. For example, they can use high-pressure water jetting to clear your pipes.

A plumber's snake is a specialized tool consisting of a flexible cable with a grabber fitting that can clear buildup and other obstructions. Many plumbers also use a motorized auger for stubborn blockages.

Initial Assessment
Clogged pipes can be a serious hassle for any resident. In addition to being inconvenient and producing unpleasant odours, they can become a safety concern if ignored.

Watch out for these indicators: bad smells, slow-draining water, and gurgling sounds from your drains.

While you might attempt some simple fixes first, serious blockages call for expert assistance. A qualified plumber may need to examine the drain clean-out and use a video inspection to find the source of the obstruction.

Plunger Solutions
A sink plunger (also known as a force cup) is a essential tool for clearing blocked drains. For maximum efficiency, start by placing the plunger under hot water to make it more flexible and improve suction.

Professional Methods
For difficult blockages, licensed technicians might use high-pressure water cleaning. This advanced technique uses high-pressure water to thoroughly clean your pipes.

Keep in mind that while drain cleaning products are available from stores, they can be harmful to your pipes. It's safer to call a licensed plumber for major clogs.
