Clogged drains are common for homeowners. If not addressed, a blocked pipe could lead to the growth of mold as well as leaks or sewage back-ups at your house, and may cause structural damage.

Sewer backups are among the worst plumbing problems homeowners can confront. They are not only smelly and ugly, they also expose residents to dangerous bacteria which can make them sick. The sewage that is soiled can damage porous surfaces like floors, drywall and furniture.

2. Clogged Drain

The drains within your home will get clogged. While a drain snake and plunger may work in certain instances, clogs which are more significant could be the sign of a blockage that is further down the line, and may require professional attention.

It's not that difficult to avoid blockages in drains by paying attention to what's going through the drains (no grease, food waste or hair) or using a strainer and keeping an eye on the drains. However, if the issue continues to persist the time is now to contact an experienced plumber.

3. Burst Pipe

We rely on pipes in order to move water from and to our taps. Pipes are susceptible to damage by freezing temperatures or blockages, as well as simply age. The pressure created by these issues could cause pipes burst.

A burst pipe can cause significant water damage to both your home and property. However, the good thing is that plumbers usually help to prevent this issue by utilizing

regular maintenance and check-ups.

4. Frozen Pipe

The winter cold temperatures are accompanied by the threat of frozen pipes. A frozen pipe can trigger various issues such as water damage or flooding. The homeowners can keep a pipe from becoming frozen by knowing the symptoms.

If you suspect frozen pipe, close your home's main water supply valve. This will lower the pressure, preventing the pipe from breaking. Shut off any faucets serviced by this pipe. This will relieve pressure and allow you to defrost the frozen section of the pipe.

Apply heat to the frozen section of pipe using the space heater, heat lamp, or hair dryer. Make use of a space heater or hair dryer in place of blowtorch or propane or the kerosene stove.

Damage to your home's water supply can be an extremely serious issue that can affect the structural integrity of your house and may require expensive repairs. The majority of water damage is resulted from leaky pipes and it can be difficult to recognize.

Water damage is caused by numerous factors like leaky pipes or flooding caused by storms or massive rainfall. Ways Professional Plumbers Can Help With a Blocked Drain

When you're dealing with clogged pipes, professional plumbers have special tools and equipment to identify and resolve the problem efficiently. For instance, they can use hydro jetting to flush your pipes.

A drain snake is a specialized tool consisting of a flexible cable with a grabber fitting that can remove buildup and other obstructions. Most plumbers also use a mechanical auger for difficult blockages.

Professional Evaluation
Blocked drains can be a serious hassle for any homeowner. In addition to being frustrating and producing unpleasant odours, they can become a safety concern if not addressed.

Be aware of these indicators: unpleasant smells, poor drainage, and unusual noises from your pipes.

While you might want to try some simple fixes first, severe blockages require expert assistance. A licensed professional may need to inspect the drain clean-out and use a camera to find the source of the obstruction.

Using a Plunger
A plunger (also known as a plumber's friend) is a simple tool for fixing blocked drains. For best results, first running the plunger under warm water to make it more flexible and improve suction.

Advanced Solutions
For stubborn blockages, professional plumbers might use water jetting. This professional method uses pressurized water to completely clear your pipes.

Note that while drain cleaning products are available from stores, they can be harsh to your pipes. It's safer to reach out to a qualified technician for major clogs.
